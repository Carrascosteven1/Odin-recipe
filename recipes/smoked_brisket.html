Smoked Brisket

A whole BBQ beef brisket (a.k.a.packer's cut brisket )is a huge hunk of cow that comes off the smoker deep ebony in color, almost black, looking more like a meteorite than a meal. But it is not burnt, and beneath the crust is the most tender, juicy, meat full of husky, beefy flavor. IF you cook it right.

  • 15-20lbs Brisket
  • Kosher Salt
  • 16 mesh Black Pepper (Coarse)
  • Worchestire (Binder)
  1. Step 1. Trim Brisket
  2. Step 2. Cover in Binder and salt and pepper brisket
  3. Step 3. Set smoker at 225 degrees
  4. Step 4. Place brisket in smoker at opposite end of fire
  5. Step 5. Pull the Brisket when the inter temp is 165
  6. Step 6. Wrap in butcher paper or foil and place back in smoker
  7. Step 7. Pull brisket at 205 or probe tender
  8. Step 8. Rest the brisket in the oven for 2 hours or overnight
